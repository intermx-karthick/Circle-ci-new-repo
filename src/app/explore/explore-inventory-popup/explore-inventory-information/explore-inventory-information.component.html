A GUIDE TO GEOPATH MEASUREMENT

Geopath is a not-for-profit tripartite measurement body for Out of Home advertising. This industry body has been providing audience and circulation measurement for over 85 years.

Geopath Impressions are the fundamental currency for buying and selling Out of Home advertising today. Impressions are not only an opportunity to see but also a likelihood to see. Many years of research went into this methodology to ensure an accurate measure of audience is provided to the marketplace.

Definitions

Inventory Specifications

Media Class - There are 4 media classes: bulletins, posters, walls & murals, and street furniture.

Media Type - There are over 50 different media types, ranging from Spectaculars, to Junior Posters, to Digital Kiosks.

Spot Dimensions - Describes the dimensions or size of the specific panel in either feet and/or inches.

Orientation - This describes the direction the media is facing. For instance, N is facing Southbound traffic.

Plant Unit ID - This identification comes specifically from the operator and can also be referred to as the Plant Panel ID.

Illumination - Written as any number between 0-24, this metric describes the number of hours/day that the media is visible.

Inventory Audience Insights

Top Contributing Zip Code (Persons: 0+) - Here we have ordered the top 5 impression-producing home geographies, by zip code.

Top Contributing DMA (Persons: 0+) - Similar to Top Zip Codes, here you will find the markets that produce the most impressions for that specific unit of inventory.

Opportunity Overview - This metric provides info as to the highest indexing Prizm Premier market segments, provided by Claritas.

Primary Attributes - These attributes including income, age, education, household composition, technology use, and ethnicity are used to personify the “typical” audience for a specific inventory.

Calculations

In-Market Total Audience Calculations

Weekly Impressions - This is the number of times a message or content are displayed and seen by a defined group of people within a viewable zone of Out of Home Media for a specific week.

Market Population - This is the population of a defined area used to contextualize buying and selling media. It can be custom-defined, or a standard geography such as DMA.

In-Market Percentage - The percentage of the total audience impressions for an out of home media location that is from all people whose home location is within a defined market geography.

In-Market Impressions - The audience impressions for an out of home media location that are from a target audience whose home location is within a defined market geography.

In-Market Target Audience Calculations

Reach - The approximate percentage of a target audience’s population who notice an advertising message at least once during an Out of Home campaign.

Frequency - The average number of times an individual notices an Out of Home advertising message during a defined period of time.

TRP - The total number of in-market impressions, to a target audience, delivered as a percentage of a market population. One rating point represents impressions equal to 1% of the market population.

Comp Index - A measure of how the in-market target audience impression composition percentage relates to the target audience composition of the market population.
